🎹 Polyphonic Piano & Sequencer

Electronics Project | EE 4020 | Spring 2025

← Back to Portfolio

Project Overview

Designed and built a fully functional 6-note polyphonic synthesizer from discrete analog components. The system combines 556 timer ICs for note generation, LM741 summing amplifier for audio mixing, and LM386 audio amplifier for speaker output. Implemented a 4017 counter for sequencer functionality with programmable melody playback.

Key Achievement: Demonstrated advanced understanding of analog electronics, circuit troubleshooting, and audio signal processing. Successfully debugged complex multi-stage circuit through 5+ hours of systematic testing.

Technical Specifications

Tone Oscillators

Six 556 timer ICs generating individual note frequencies via voltage dividers

Audio Mixing

LM741 op-amp summing amplifier combining all note signals

Audio Output

LM386 audio amplifier, speaker driver, volume control

Sequencer

4017 decade counter for tempo control, programmable sequences

Tempo Control

555 timer-based clock generator with variable frequency

Power Supply

±12V dual supply, decoupling capacitors for stability

Key Features

  • Polyphonic Synthesis: Play up to 6 notes simultaneously
  • Programmable Sequencer: Create and play pre-programmed melodies
  • Variable Tempo: Adjust playback speed via 555 timer frequency
  • Volume Control: Adjustable output level
  • Full Discrete Analog Design: No microcontrollers or digital components

Circuit Architecture

Stage 1: Oscillator Bank (556 Timers)

Each tone oscillator uses a 556 timer IC in astable mode. Frequency selection achieved through digital logic gates controlling which capacitor/resistor pairs are active. This enables selection of musical notes (C, D, E, F, G, A).

Stage 2: Summing Amplifier (LM741)

All six oscillator outputs feed into an inverting summing amplifier. The LM741 combines signals while maintaining individual amplitude control. Output impedance matched to prevent signal loading.

Stage 3: Output Amplifier (LM386)

Audio signal from summing amplifier feeds into LM386 power amplifier for driving speaker. Includes volume control potentiometer and feedback network for stability.

Stage 4: Sequencer (4017 Counter)

4017 decade counter driven by 555 timer clock. Each output selects a different note or rest. Creates repeating sequences up to 10 steps long.

Design Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Signal Distortion

Solution: Added summing resistors to prevent loading, optimized impedance matching between stages

Challenge: Frequency Drift

Solution: Used precision resistors, added temperature compensation, used regulated power supply

Challenge: Oscillation in Amplifiers

Solution: Added compensation capacitors, optimized layout to reduce feedback paths
