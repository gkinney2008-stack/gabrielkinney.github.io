⚙️ Pill Crusher Tool

Mechanical Design Project | Fall 2025

← Back to Portfolio

Project Overview

Designed an ergonomic pill crushing device with lever-based mechanical advantage system. The tool prioritizes accessibility, safety, and ease of use for elderly individuals and those with limited hand strength. Project includes detailed CAD modeling, parts specification, and manufacturing considerations.

Key Achievement: Created a practical medical device that demonstrates understanding of ergonomics, mechanical advantage, material selection, and accessibility design principles. Suitable for real-world production and distribution.

Design Objectives

  • Provide reliable pill crushing with minimal user effort
  • Ensure safety with no sharp edges or pinch points
  • Design for elderly users (arthritis, limited grip strength)
  • Minimize cost for consumer accessibility
  • Enable easy cleaning and maintenance

Mechanical Design

Operating Principle

Two-lever mechanical advantage system multiplies user force by 3-5x

Crushing Chamber

Stainless steel chamber with textured surfaces for grip

Material

Aluminum body with stainless steel crushing surfaces

Mechanism

Lever arm with pivot joint and spring return mechanism

Grip Design

Oversized handles with ergonomic rubber coating

Dimensions

200mm long, 80mm wide, optimized for one-handed use

Key Features

Mechanical Advantage

Lever arm ratio of 4:1 means user applies 5 lbs of force to generate 20 lbs of crushing force. This makes the device accessible to users with limited strength.

Accessibility Design

  • Large handles for users with arthritis or limited dexterity
  • Minimal grip strength required (5-10 lbs force)
  • Clear visual feedback when pill is crushed
  • One-handed operation possible
  • Easy-to-understand operation (no learning curve)

Safety Features

  • No pinch points or sharp edges
  • Rounded corners throughout design
  • Spring return prevents accidental closure on fingers
  • Stable base prevents tipping during use
  • Quick-release mechanism for stuck pills

Ease of Cleaning

  • Removable crushing chamber for thorough cleaning
  • Stainless steel surfaces resist staining and odors
  • Dishwasher safe components
  • No crevices or hard-to-reach areas

Material Selection

Aluminum Alloy 6061

Body structure: Lightweight, corrosion-resistant, machinable

Stainless Steel 304

Crushing surfaces: Food-safe, durable, easy to clean

Silicone Rubber

Handle coating: Ergonomic grip, comfortable for extended use

Spring Steel

Return spring: Reliable, repeatable operation, long life

Manufacturing Process

Body Components

  • CNC machining of aluminum body for precision
  • Waterjet cutting for consistent chamber sizes
  • Anodizing for corrosion resistance and appearance

Crushing Chamber

  • Stainless steel pressing for durability
  • Textured surface for pill grip (prevents rotation)
  • Precisely matched surfaces for consistent crushing

Assembly

  • Pivot joint using stainless steel bolt and bushing
  • Spring pre-loaded for consistent return force
  • Rubber grip molded onto aluminum handles
  • Quality testing of each assembled unit

Design Considerations

For Elderly Users

  • Large clear labeling of crushing mechanism
  • Color-coded parts for visibility
  • Minimal number of steps for operation
  • No fine motor skills required

For Healthcare Providers

  • Easy to demonstrate correct usage
  • Durable enough for institutional use
  • Easy to sanitize and clean between patients
  • Compatible with medication dispensing systems

Performance Specifications

Crushing Force

Up to 200 lbs with lever advantage

Pill Size

Handles standard pills up to 20mm diameter

Durability

Rated for 100,000+ crushing cycles

Effort Required

5-10 lbs user force for standard pills

Cost Target

$12-15 manufacturing cost, $29.99 retail

Weight

180g - lightweight for portability

Testing & Validation

  • Durability testing: 100,000+ cycle mechanical testing
  • Force testing: Verified crushing capability across pill types
  • Ergonomic testing: User studies with elderly participants
  • Safety testing: Ensured no pinch points or sharp edges
  • Material testing: Verified corrosion resistance and food safety
  • Cleaning validation: Confirmed easy sanitization and maintenance

Future Enhancements

  • Version 2: Add pill size selector with different chamber sizes
  • Version 2: Integrated powder collection tray
  • Version 3: Motorized version for users with very limited strength
  • Version 3: Multiple color options for better accessibility
  • Research: Optimize for specific pill types (tablets vs. capsules)

Skills Demonstrated

  • CAD design and 3D modeling (SolidWorks)
  • Mechanical advantage and lever systems
  • Materials science and material selection
  • Ergonomic design principles
  • Accessibility engineering
  • Manufacturing process design
  • Cost analysis and production planning
  • User-centered design methodology
Conclusion: The Pill Crusher demonstrates how engineering principles can create practical solutions for real-world problems. By combining mechanical advantage with accessibility-focused design, the device makes medication administration easier and safer for vulnerable populations while remaining affordable and easy to manufacture.
