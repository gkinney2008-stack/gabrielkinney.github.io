Pill Crusher Tool

Accessibility engineering · Lever mechanics · CAD design

Overview

Why a pill crusher?

Medication is vital for the sick and elderly to live longer lives. Millions of people are plagued with issues that make medication intake a difficult process. An accessible pill crusher allows users with varying disabilities to independently access their medication.

Problem Statement

Who is this for?

Dysphagia
Difficulty Swallowing

Up to 40% of American adults have difficulty swallowing pills, with age being a major risk factor due to increased medication use. (Harvard Health)

Arthritis
Reduced Hand Strength

48.8% of the elderly have decreased hand grip strength, and ~50% have some degree of arthritis — making standard pill crushers difficult or impossible to use. (EEAN)

Vision
Sight Issues / Blindness

1.5 million elderly people in the US are classified as blind, and over 4.2 million Americans are visually impaired. (CDC)

Design Solutions

How we addressed each problem.

Pill Crusher + Pill Tray Increases medication accessibility for those with dysphagia. A slide-out tray makes cleanup simple once the medication is crushed.
Lever-Based System & Ergonomics Uses mechanical advantage to reduce the force required to crush medication — accessible to users with arthritis and hand mobility issues.
Braille & Large Text Braille and large text labeling allows visually impaired users to independently access and operate the device.
Design Justification

Engineering rationale.

  • Why a lever? A lever-based model uses mechanical advantage to reduce the force required to crush a pill. An ergonomic grip allows for a more comfortable and accessible user experience, as the handle molds to the hand.
  • Why a pill tray? The pill tray was added to make it easy to remove the crushed pill. This allows for easy cleanup and extraction without mess or spillage.
  • Why Braille? Braille was added to the design to help the visually impaired who need to have crushed medication independently access and use the device.
